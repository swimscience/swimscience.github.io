Understanding Training Zones

Training zones divide your swimming intensity into 5 distinct levels, each targeting specific physiological adaptations. Proper zone training maximizes results while minimizing injury risk.

Zone 1: Recovery (60-70% effort)

Easy swimming that promotes active recovery and aerobic base building. Use for warmups, cooldowns, and recovery days. Should feel effortless and sustainable indefinitely.

Zone 2: Aerobic Endurance (70-80% effort)

The foundation of swimming fitness. Builds aerobic capacity, improves fat metabolism, and increases mitochondrial density. This is your "all day" pace - comfortably hard where conversation is possible.

Zone 3: Threshold (80-90% effort)

Training at lactate threshold - the boundary between aerobic and anaerobic work. This is typically your Critical Swim Speed (CSS) or the pace you can hold for 20-30 minutes maximum. Feels "comfortably hard" but sustainable for extended intervals.

Zone 4: VO2 Max (90-95% effort)

High-intensity intervals that improve maximum oxygen uptake. These are hard efforts lasting 2-8 minutes with substantial rest between. Builds speed and power but requires significant recovery.

Zone 5: Anaerobic (95-100% effort)

Sprint training at near-maximum or maximum effort. Develops explosive power and speed. Used sparingly due to high fatigue and recovery demands. Typically 15 seconds to 2 minutes maximum.

Training Applications

Use these zones to structure your weekly training. A balanced program includes 70% Zone 1-2 (base building), 20% Zone 3-4 (threshold and VO2), and 10% Zone 5 (speed work).

Testing Your Zones

Test zones every 4-6 weeks using a CSS test (400m + 200m time trials) or recent race times. As fitness improves, your pace at each zone will get faster - update your training paces accordingly.
