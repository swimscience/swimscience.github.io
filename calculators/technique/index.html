Why Technique Matters

Technique is the foundation of swimming performance. Small improvements in efficiency can dramatically reduce your pace per 100m without increasing effort.

The efficiency equation: Speed = Stroke Length × Stroke Rate

Most swimmers focus on stroke rate (turnover) but neglect stroke length. Proper technique maximizes distance per stroke, requiring fewer strokes and less energy for the same speed.

Technique Training Principles

The 80/20 Rule for Technique

Spend 80% of technique training on fundamentals (body position, catch, timing) and 20% on refinement (small adjustments, race-specific technique).

Progressive Overload

Improve technique gradually:

  1. Slow speed, perfect technique: Master mechanics at easy pace
  2. Moderate speed, good technique: Maintain form at aerobic pace
  3. Race pace, solid technique: Keep fundamentals at high intensity

Video Analysis

The single best tool for technique improvement is video analysis. Record yourself from side and underwater views to identify errors you can't feel.

Measuring Technique Progress

Track these metrics to quantify technique improvements:

  • Stroke Count per 25m: Lower is generally more efficient. Target: Under 20 (freestyle), Under 22 (backstroke), 6-8 (breaststroke), 8-12 (butterfly)
  • SWOLF Score: Time + strokes for 25m. Lower is better. Use our SWOLF Calculator
  • Critical Swim Speed (CSS): Your threshold pace reflects overall efficiency. Track with CSS Calculator
  • Pace per 100m: Monitor improvements across all strokes with Pace Calculator
